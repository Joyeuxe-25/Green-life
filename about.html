Who We Are

Community-Focused Environmental Action

Green for Life Rwanda (GLR) is an environmental organization that empowers communities, particularly small household farmers, to conserve the environment and improve livelihoods. Our work is centered on practical, community-driven action that connects environmental protection with real improvements in people's daily lives.

We believe that lasting environmental change only happens when communities are at the center of it — as participants, decision-makers, and beneficiaries. That is the approach that guides everything we do.

Our Foundation

What Drives Us

What We Stand For

We stand for environmental conservation that is grounded in community life. Our work encourages environmental responsibility, strengthens community participation, and supports livelihood improvement — connecting nature and people in a shared future.

Our Purpose

Our purpose is to empower communities in environmental protection while improving livelihoods. We give particular attention to small household farmers — supporting their work, building their knowledge, and connecting them to sustainable practices that strengthen their resilience.

Our Approach

How We Work

Community Empowerment

Our work is centered on empowering communities through environmental action — including saving groups, clubs, and participatory training programs.

Environmental Protection

We focus on conserving the environment through practical local engagement — tree planting, agroforestry, watershed protection, and sustainable land use.

Support for Farmers

We give special attention to small household farmers and their livelihoods — training them in agroforestry, compost making, and sustainable agricultural practices.

Sustainable Livelihoods

We connect environmental work with improved livelihoods — ensuring that conservation efforts translate into real economic and social benefits for communities.

Our History

Our Story

Green for Life Rwanda was founded in 2017 with a clear mission: to empower communities to conserve the environment while improving their livelihoods. From its beginnings, the organization has focused on practical, ground-level environmental work with small household farmers.

Since its founding, GLR has proceeded with legal registration and has grown its programs to include environmental clubs, saving groups, agroforestry training, tree nurseries, compost making, and youth environmental education.
