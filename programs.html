Our Programs

Practical environmental action that empowers communities and strengthens livelihoods.

How We Work

Green for Life Rwanda partners with communities to implement programs that integrate environmental conservation with livelihood improvement. Through hands-on training, community mobilization, and sustainable practices, we help people become active stewards of their environment while building a better future for themselves and their families.

Main Program Areas

🌳

Agroforestry

Promoting tree planting alongside crops to restore soil health, increase biodiversity, and provide additional income through fruit, timber, and firewood.

  • Fruit tree nurseries
  • Boundary planting
  • Soil conservation
🌍

Climate Action

Helping communities adapt to climate change through reforestation, water conservation, and sustainable land management practices.

  • Tree planting campaigns
  • Rainwater harvesting
  • Climate-smart agriculture
📚

Environmental Education & Youth Clubs

Engaging young people through school-based environmental clubs, workshops, and activities that build awareness and leadership.

  • School tree planting
  • Waste management projects
  • Environmental camps
👩‍🌾

Community Livelihoods & Advocacy

Empowering smallholder farmers, especially women, with training in sustainable agriculture, income-generating activities, and advocacy for environmental rights.

  • Compost making
  • Organic pesticides
  • Saving groups support

Hands‑on Activities

Compost Making

Compost Production

Training farmers to turn organic waste into nutrient-rich compost, improving soil fertility and reducing reliance on chemical fertilizers.

Pesticides Making

Natural Pesticides

Workshops on preparing botanical pesticides from local plants, offering a low-cost, eco-friendly alternative.

Tree Nurseries

Tree Nursery Management

Community-run nurseries produce seedlings for reforestation and agroforestry, creating local employment and ownership.

Farmer Field Schools

Farmer Training

Hands-on learning groups where farmers experiment with and adopt sustainable techniques together.

Who We Support

👨‍🌾

Smallholder Farmers

Men and women who depend on farming for their livelihoods, receiving training and resources to improve yields sustainably.

🧒

Youth & Students

Young people engaged through environmental clubs, learning to become future environmental leaders.

🏘️

Local Communities

Entire communities benefit from restored ecosystems, improved water sources, and collective action.

👩

Women's Groups

Special focus on empowering women through saving groups, leadership opportunities, and livelihood activities.

Integrated Approach

Our programs are designed to reinforce each other. Environmental education builds awareness that leads to community-led conservation. Agroforestry provides food and income while restoring land. Livelihood activities reduce pressure on natural resources. Together, they create a cycle of sustainability and empowerment.
